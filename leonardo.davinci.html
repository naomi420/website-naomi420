Leonardo da Vinci

Leonardo di ser Piero da Vinci, known as Leonardo da Vinci, was an Italian polymath of the Renaissance whose areas of interest included:

  • invention,
  • drawing,
  • painting,
  • sculpture,
  • architecture,
  • science,
  • music,
  • mathematics,
  • engineering,
  • literature,
  • anatomy,
  • astronomy,
  • botany,
  • paleontology,
  • and cartography.

His natural genius crossed so many disciplines that he epitomized the term “Renaissance man.” Today he remains best known for his art, including two paintings that remain among the world's most famous and admired, Mona Lisa and The Last Supper. Art, da Vinci believed, was indisputably connected with science and nature. And he might be the most well known painters around the globe.
